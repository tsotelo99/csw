# 2026 C. Ruth and Calvin P. Horn Lecturer

{% include figure.html class="left" width="45%" caption="Dr. Elizabeth Ellis" image-path="/assets/images/people/ellis-headshot.jpg" %}

The Center for the Southwest at the University of New Mexico is pleased to announce the 2026 C. Ruth and Calvin P. HornLecture in Western History and Culture, which will take place Thursday, March 26, 2026, from 5:30pm to 7:00 pm in Hodgin Hall, Bobo Room. 

The lecture will be given by Dr. Elizabeth Ellis, Associate Professor of History at Princeton University and a citizen of the Peoria Tribe of Indians of Oklahoma. Dr. Ellis teaches early American and Native American history. She is a scholar of early North America with a focus on diplomacy, borderlands, cross-cultural exchange, and Indigenous politics. Her first book, The Great Power of Small Nations: Indigenous Diplomacy in the Gulf South (2022), examined the formation of Native American nations in the Lower Mississippi Valley. 

If you need a parkign pass or more details about the event, please contact us at cntws@unm.edu
